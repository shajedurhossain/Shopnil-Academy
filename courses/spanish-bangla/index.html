What you'll learn in A1

From "¡Hola!" to your first real conversation.

By the end of A1, you'll be able to introduce yourself, ask basic questions, and survive in everyday Spanish situations.

    • ✓The Spanish alphabet (el alfabeto), pronunciation, and unique sounds (ñ, rr, j, ll)
    • ✓Greetings, introductions, and basic small talk (Hola, ¿Cómo estás?, ¿Qué tal?)
    • ✓Present tense (presente) — the foundation of all Spanish conversation
    • ✓Definite & indefinite articles (el, la, los, las, un, una)
    • ✓Numbers 0–1000, dates, times, days, and months
    • ✓Family, food, daily routine — common A1 vocabulary themes
    • ✓Asking questions — qué, quién, cuándo, dónde, cómo, por qué
    • ✓Ordering at cafés, restaurants, and shops
    • ✓Asking for directions and giving simple location descriptions
    • ✓500+ essential A1 words mapped with Bangla equivalents
    • ✓Ser vs. estar — the first big "Spanish puzzle," explained simply
    • ✓Mistakes Bangla speakers make — and how to avoid them
